When the Footsteps Fade

Category: Poetry | Posted on 13 Sep 2025

The moment has come, my eyes have closed,
A world once bright, now dim and cold.
They lay me down, so still, so deep,
And whisper prayers as loved ones weep.

I hear their sobs, I feel their pain,
But soon, they turn and walk away.
Their footsteps echo, soft then light,
Fading slowly out of sight.

I spent my life for them, I see,
Chased their love so endlessly.
I sought their smiles, feared their frowns,
Yet now they leave without a sound.

O heart, you loved this world too much,
But see how little stays in touch?
The hands I held, the laughs I shared,
Are gone—and now, I'm left aware.

Two angels come, their voices near,
A question asked, so loud, so clear.
"Who is your Lord?" they ask of me,
"What did you say of this man—Muhammad? Tell me."

O tongue, don’t fail, O heart, don’t shake,
For now, my deeds decide my fate.
No wealth remains, no worldly prize,
Just what I sent for my afterlife.

O soul, awake! While breath remains,
This world is short, its joys will fade.
Turn to Allah, before you’re laid,
Before your footsteps, too, will fade.
